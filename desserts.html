Traditional Desserts

Cendol

Shaved ice dessert with green rice flour jelly, coconut milk, and palm sugar syrup. Often topped with red beans and sweet corn.

Ais Kacang

Also known as ABC (Air Batu Campur), this dessert features shaved ice with red beans, grass jelly, sweet corn, and various syrups.

Cultural Context

Malaysian desserts and snacks reflect the country's diverse cultural heritage, with influences from Malay, Chinese, and Indian traditions. Many desserts are based on rice, coconut, and palm sugar - ingredients abundant in the region.

Street food culture is an integral part of Malaysian life, with night markets (pasar malam) offering a wide variety of affordable snacks and sweets. Sharing food is an important social activity, and many desserts are associated with specific festivals and celebrations.
