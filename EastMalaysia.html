TOP 10 MUST TRY FOOD IN EAST MALAYSIA

Discover the unique culinary treasures of East Malaysia

Sarawak Laksa
★★★★★

A signature Sarawak noodle soup with a rich, spicy broth made from sambal belacan, sour tamarind, and coconut milk.

Kolo Mee
★★★★★

Dry noodles tossed in a savory sauce with minced pork, char siu, and spring onions. A Sarawak favorite.

Beef Noodle
★★★★☆

Hearty noodles served with tender beef slices, beef balls, and a flavorful broth.

Nasi Linopot
★★★★☆

Traditional Kadazan-Dusun rice wrapped in leaves, often served with grilled fish or chicken.

Umai
★★★★☆

A Melanau dish of raw fish marinated in lime juice, onions, chilies, and salt. Similar to ceviche.

Pansuh
★★★★☆

Meat (usually chicken) cooked in bamboo with herbs and spices. A traditional Dayak cooking method.

Hinava
★★★★☆

A Kadazan-Dusun dish of raw fish cured in lime juice with ginger, chili, and bitter gourd.

Mee Tuaran
★★★★☆

Freshly made noodles from Tuaran town, stir-fried with eggs, vegetables, and meat.

Linopot
★★★★☆

Sticky rice wrapped in leaves, a traditional indigenous food often eaten during festivals.

Kek Lapis Sarawak
★★★★★

Sarawak's famous layered cake with intricate patterns and flavors. A must-try dessert.
